Theorem 1 (Consistency of MLEs) Assume that \({X}_{1}, \dots, {X}_{n}\) satisfy the subsequent regularity conditions:

  1. \({X}_{1},{X}_{2}, \dots ,{X}_{n}\) are observed, where \({X}_{i} \sim f \left( x \, | \, \theta \right)\) are independent and identically distributed (IID).

  2. The parameter is identifiable; that is, if \(\theta \neq {\theta}'\), then \(f \left( x \, | \, \theta \right) \neq f \left( x \, | \, {\theta}' \right)\).

  3. The densities \(f \left( x \, | \, \theta \right)\) have common support, and \(f \left( x \, | \, \theta \right)\) is differentiable with respect to \(\theta\) in \(\Theta\).

  4. The parameter space \(\Theta\) contains an open set \(\omega\) of which the true parameter value \({\theta}_{0}\) is an interior point.

Then the likelihood equation, \[\begin{equation} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} L \left (\theta \right) = 0, \end{equation}\] or equivalently \[\begin{equation} \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} \ell \left (\theta \right) = 0, \end{equation}\] has a solution \(\widehat{\theta }_{n}\) such that \(\widehat{\theta }_{n}\stackrel{p}{\to} \theta\).
Proof. Let \(\delta > 0\) such that \(\left ({\theta}_{0} - \delta \, , \, {\theta}_{0} + \delta \right) \subseteq \omega\). Define, \[\begin{equation} {A}_{n , \delta} = \left \{ \mathbf{X} \, : \, \ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right ) \, > \, \ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, - \, \delta \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right ) \right \}. \end{equation}\] \[\begin{equation} {B}_{n , \delta} = \left \{ \mathbf{X} \, : \, \ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right ) \, > \, \ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, + \, \delta \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right ) \right \}. \end{equation}\] where \(\mathbf{X} = \left ( {X}_{1}, \dots, {X}_{n} \right)\). Let \({S}_{n , \delta} = {A}_{n , \delta} \, \cap \, {B}_{n , \delta}\). Hereby, \[\begin{equation} \begin{split} 1 & \geq P \left ( {S}_{n , \delta} \right ) \\ & = P \left ( {A}_{n , \delta} \, \cap \, {B}_{n , \delta} \right ) \\ & = P \left ({\left ( {A}^{\complement}_{n , \delta} \, \cup \, {B}^{\complement}_{n , \delta} \right )}^{\complement} \right ) \\ & = 1 - P \left ( {A}^{\complement}_{n , \delta} \, \cup \, {B}^{\complement}_{n , \delta} \right ) \\ & \geq 1 - P \left ( {A}^{\complement}_{n , \delta} \right ) - P \left ( {B}^{\complement}_{n , \delta} \right ) \\ & = P \left ( {A}_{n , \delta} \right ) + P \left ( {B}_{n , \delta} \right ) - 1. \end{split} \end{equation}\] Subsequently, \[\begin{equation} 1 \geq \lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( {S}_{n , \delta} \right )} \geq \lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( {A}_{n , \delta} \right )} + \lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( {B}_{n , \delta} \right )} - 1. \end{equation}\] Thus, \(1 \geq \lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( {S}_{n , \delta} \right )} \geq 1\) and, therefore, \(\lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( {S}_{n , \delta} \right )} = 1\). Furthermore, let is prove that \({S}_{n , \delta} \subseteq \left \{ \mathbf{X} \, : \, \left | \, \widehat{\theta }_{n} \, - \, {\theta}_{0} \, \right | \, < \, \delta \right \}\). Assume that \(\mathbf{X} \in {S}_{n , \delta}\), then \(\mathbf{X} \in {A}_{n , \delta} \, \wedge \, \mathbf{X} \in {B}_{n , \delta}\), namely, \(\ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right ) \, > \, \ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, - \, \delta \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right ) \, \wedge \, \ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right ) \, > \, \ell \left ( {\theta}_{0} \, + \, \delta \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right )\). Since \(\ell \left ( \theta \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right )\) is differentiable on \(\omega\), then, it is differentiable and, in consequence, continuous on \(\left ({\theta}_{0} - \delta \, , \, {\theta}_{0} + \delta \right)\). Hence, \(\ell \left ( \theta \, | \, \mathbf{X} \right )\) has a local maximum on this interval, \(\widehat{\theta }_{n}\), by hypothesis. In this manner, \[\begin{equation} {\theta}_{0} - \delta < \widehat{\theta }_{n} < {\theta}_{0} + \delta \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad \left | \, \widehat{\theta }_{n} \, - \, {\theta}_{0} \, \right | < \delta. \end{equation}\] This validates that \({S}_{n , \delta} \subseteq \left \{ \mathbf{X} \, : \, \left | \, \widehat{\theta }_{n} \, - \, {\theta}_{0} \, \right | \, < \, \delta \right \}\). Thence, \[\begin{equation} 1 = \lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( {S}_{n , \delta} \right )} \leq \lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( \left | \, \widehat{\theta }_{n} \, - \, {\theta}_{0} \, \right | < \delta \right )} \leq 1. \end{equation}\] As a result, \(\lim _{n \, \to \, \infty}{P \left ( \left | \, \widehat{\theta }_{n} \, - \, {\theta}_{0} \, \right | < \delta \right )} = 1\), i.e., \(\widehat{\theta }_{n}\stackrel{p}{\to} \theta\). \(\square\)
